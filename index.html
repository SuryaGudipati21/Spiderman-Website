Who Is Spider-Man?

Spider-Man is a fictional superhero created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko for Marvel Comics. His real name is Peter Parker, a teenager from New York City who gains superhuman abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider.

His powers include enhanced strength, agility, wall-climbing, and a special “spider-sense” that warns him of danger. After the tragic death of his Uncle Ben, Peter learns that with great power comes great responsibility and chooses to fight crime as Spider-Man.

Spider-Man's Powers

  • Superhuman strength (up to the 10-ton range in some comics)
  • Enhanced reflexes and reaction time
  • Spider-Sense (precognitive danger awareness)
  • Increased durability and stamina
  • Accelerated healing
